Joaquim Maria Machado de Assis ( Portuguese: [ʒwɐˈkĩ maˈɾiɐ maˈʃadu d͡ʒ(i) aˈsis] ), often known by his surnames as Machado de Assis , Machado, or Bruxo do Cosme Velho (21 June 1839 – 29 September 1908), was a pioneer Brazilian novelist, poet, playwright and short story writer, widely regarded as the greatest writer of Brazilian literature . In 1897, he founded and became the first President of the Brazilian Academy of Letters . He was multilingual, having taught himself French, English, German and Greek later in life. Born in Morro do Livramento [ pt ] , Rio de Janeiro , from a poor family, he was the grandson of freed slaves in a country where slavery would not be fully abolished until 49 years later. He barely studied in public schools and never attended university.
